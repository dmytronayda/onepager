Seeking Sierra

Seeking Sierra is an immersive experience that brings together elements of storytelling, game principles, and role-playing. Seeking Sierra moves students out of the textbook, and into immersive and active learning environments by combining virtual reality and instructor support.

Game-Like Learning Principles

Seeking Sierra is an immersive experience that brings together elements of storytelling, game principles, and role-playing.

Learn more

In Seeking Sierra, ESL students assume the role of new recruits in an secret agency and are tasked to unravel a plot in a way never seen before…

Students and language instructors will participate in creating a story. Students and instructors will collaboratively solve problems – working together in classroom discussions, participating in immersive virtual reality experiences, and completing out-of-class activities that extend the narrative into real life situations.

Safe spaces to experiment:

Practicing a new language can be intimidating.

Seeking Sierra utilizes virtual reality, providing a private, low stakes, immersive experience. Students are able to experiment with their language skills, make mistakes, and practice, all while participating in an active learning environment.

Collaborative problem solving:

Language learning materials are frequently disconnected activities that are… boring!

Seeking Sierra employs activities that are designed as collaborative puzzles. This provides motivation for championing the language skill at hand, as well as providing impetus for group collaboration.

Cross-modality game play:

Language learning can be difficult to apply in real-life situations.

Seeking Sierra is built on a single, cohesive narrative to engage students. The story requires input from the student, and is focused on everyday-life locations and scenarios, making immediate application a very natural transition.

Narrative

  • It provides a call to action for students (discovery learning)
  • It is a widespread theme, and familiar across many cultures
  • It is adaptable for longer-term storylines
  • It provides an intriguing context for real-life situations, including going through customs, hotel reception, restaurant scenarios, and networking events.

The instructor takes on a role as a member of the spy agency, and lets students know in the first meeting that they’ve been recruited to help out with a crisis situation.

Audience & Pedagogy

Audience:

Designed for B1+ - B2 levels students, 16 and up.

Pedagogy:

Seeking Sierra integrates learning objectives throughout the experience aligned to the Common European Framework.

Grammar Learning Objectives:

In the virtual reality mission, students will receive exposure to different sets of grammar structures, and practice them in their responses as they participate in the narrative. Instructors receive a log based on student performance in the game, that enable discussion and clarification around grammar points where students are struggling.

Listening Learning Objectives:

Students practice listening and comprehension in the virtual reality missions, the instructor-led sessions, as well as suggested homework opportunities.

Reading Learning Objectives:

Virtual reality missions provide opportunities for students to practice reading, and highlight level-appropriate primary learning objectives. Students must also utilize reading skills in homework, to gain information that will be necessary for moving forward with future missions.

Writing Learning Objectives:

Suggested homework activities that are integrated with the narrative provide contextualized experience with writing learning objectives, that have practical applications. This include, writing for presentations, memos, articles, emails, and similar application-based writing.

Speaking Learning Objectives

Student-teacher interactions and discussions provided in the supplemental materials focus on providing students with opportunities to practice speaking.

Session Structure:

Classes take place in person, with one instructor and two students.

Each 1-hour session includes 5 stages, where students interact with the instructor to prepare and recap their virtual reality experiences (T-S), and participate in virtual reality missions.

FAQ

What do I need to do to have my ESL school join the pilot?

Contact us at email[at]gmail.com

To join the pilot you’ll need to schedule session demo time. The session demo can be scheduled for a 1.5 hour session or a 2.5 hour session. Seeking Sierra provides experienced ESL schools that will go to your school, set up and run the session.

Who are the instructors?

For the Seeking Sierra pilot, we provide experienced ESL teachers to run the sessions for students interested in the program. Instructors from the school interested in observing the program are welcome to join.

Seeking Sierra is designed to be used with the instructors at your school and comes with materials that walk through the program design. We additionally provide instructor training and support through consultations and demos.

What's the pricing model?

The pilot is free.

After the startup session, Seeking Sierra will be offered as a subscription model to schools.

What happens after the pilot?

After the pilot, we’ll incorporate feedback into our development and keep you up to date with future chapter rollouts. We’ll be completing a Season, with content available for 3 months.

My school has large class sizes, can I still use Seeking Sierra?

The initial version of Seeking Sierra will support classes of up to 4 students per instructor, and support for 8 students per instructor is planned shortly after.

We’re also exploring ways to support independent product use that will require lower student-teacher interaction time.
